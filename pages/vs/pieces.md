# Mirror vs Pieces: Memory for Developers vs. Memory for Everyone

Pieces is genuinely thoughtful product design. It captures code snippets, workflow patterns, and development context directly in your IDE. For engineers, it's powerful—you're capturing the actual artifacts of your work (code, configurations, design patterns) and making them searchable and reusable. The team understood their audience well.

The insight behind Pieces is sound: your AI needs context about the work you do. The difference is *where* you do that work and *what* context matters.

## The Gap: IDE Memory vs. Workflow Memory

Pieces lives in your development environment (VS Code, JetBrains, etc.). It's built for engineers. It captures code, configuration, patterns—the artifacts of software development.

Most people don't work in an IDE. They work in email, calendars, meetings, Slack, documents. They're knowledge workers, founders, managers, researchers. They have the same fundamental need as developers—their AI should understand their context. But their context lives in a completely different place.

Mirror captures context from where you actually work: your email, your calendar, your meetings, your relationships. We build a knowledge graph of your commitments, priorities, and relationships. Same insight as Pieces (AI needs your context), completely different domain.

## How Mirror Fills the Gap

**1. Built for knowledge workers, not developers**
Pieces was designed for engineers in their IDE. Mirror was designed for everyone else—founders, managers, researchers, marketers, salespeople. People who work in email, meetings, and conversations, not code editors.

**2. Captures relationships, not code**
Pieces captures code snippets and development patterns. Mirror captures relationships and commitments. When you're about to call your investor, Mirror reminds you of what she cares about and what you committed to. That's the knowledge worker equivalent of a developer having their code library at hand.

**3. Proactive briefings, not on-demand snippets**
Pieces is retrieval-based—you search for that pattern you remember using. Mirror is proactive—before your meeting, Mirror briefes you. You don't have to remember to search for context. We surface what matters.

**4. Works across your entire toolkit**
Pieces integrates with IDEs. Mirror works with ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini—the AI tools where knowledge workers spend their time. Your memory is available wherever you're working.

**5. Captures workflow, not code**
Developers care about how to solve technical problems. Knowledge workers care about their commitments, relationships, and priorities. Pieces helps developers remember how to do things. Mirror helps everyone else remember who to talk to, what they committed to, and what actually matters.

**6. Not for technical setup**
Pieces requires IDE integration and developer setup. Mirror works in 90 seconds—connect email, connect calendar, done. You don't need a technical team to activate it.

## Which One Is Right for You?

**Choose Pieces if:**
- You're a software developer
- You work primarily in an IDE (VS Code, JetBrains, etc.)
- You need to reference code patterns and snippets
- You want to capture your development workflow
- Your team benefits from shared code context

**Choose Mirror if:**
- You're a knowledge worker, founder, manager, or researcher
- You use AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini)
- You need context before meetings
- Your work happens in email, meetings, and conversations
- You want memory that's proactive, not search-based
- You care about understanding relationships and commitments

## The Bottom Line

Pieces and Mirror are solving the same underlying problem: your AI should understand the context of your work. But they're solving it for completely different people in completely different domains.

Pieces says: "Engineers need their AI to understand code." That's right. Mirror says: "Everyone else needs their AI to understand relationships and commitments." That's equally right.

You don't need to choose between them. If you're a developer who also needs personal memory (and most founders do), you can use both. Pieces for your code. Mirror for your relationships.

But if you're not a developer, Pieces won't help you. Mirror is built for you.
