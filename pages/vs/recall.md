# Mirror vs Microsoft Recall: Privacy-First vs. Surveillance

Microsoft Recall had a clear goal: make everything on your screen searchable. But it crashed into a wall of privacy concerns. The product screenshots everything you do, stores it locally, and makes it searchable. When people understood what Recall was actually doing, they rejected it—hard. Microsoft delayed the feature twice and fundamentally rearchitected how it works.

The lesson is simple: people don't want surveillance. They want *understanding*.

## The Gap: Monitoring Everything vs. Understanding What Matters

Recall's approach is comprehensive capture. Screenshots of everything. Then search. The problem isn't just privacy—though that's real and justified. The problem is that *screenshots aren't the right data for AI*.

Your AI doesn't need a screenshot of your screen. It needs to understand your relationships. It needs to know your commitments. It needs to know what matters to you and the people you work with.

Mirror doesn't capture screenshots. We capture structure. Your calendar, your email, your commitments. We build a knowledge graph of the relationships, topics, and decisions that actually matter. That's both more useful *and* more private.

## How Mirror Fills the Gap

**1. Structured data, not surveillance**
Recall captures images of your entire screen. Mirror captures structured data—email, calendar, relationships. We're not monitoring you. We're understanding you. That's both better for AI and better for privacy.

**2. Built security-first, not patched later**
Recall's privacy problems weren't accidental—they were baked into the architecture. Taking screenshots of everything and storing them locally is inherently risky. Mirror was built security-first. End-to-end encryption. No local storage of sensitive data. Privacy isn't a feature we added after launch; it's foundational.

**3. Relational memory, not image search**
Recall finds screenshots. "I remember seeing that on my screen." Mirror understands context. "I remember that you committed to reviewing Sarah's product roadmap, and she prioritizes shipping the API layer." That's the difference between surveillance and intelligence.

**4. Opt-in, not opt-out**
Recall started as opt-out (defaulting to on)—you had to actively disable surveillance. Mirror is opt-in, you intentionally grant access. And we only access what we tell you we're accessing: email, calendar, meeting transcripts. Not your entire screen. Not your files. Just what you choose to share.

**5. No device bloat**
Recall stores screenshots locally, consuming massive amounts of storage. Mirror syncs lightweight structured data—kilobytes instead of gigabytes. Your device isn't dragged down by video files of everything you've done. You get better memory with less storage overhead.

**6. Portable, not locked to Windows**
Recall is Windows-only (at least as designed). Mirror works across your devices and with all your AI tools. Your memory follows you.

## Which One Is Right for You?

**Choose Recall if:**
- You want to search for screenshots of things you've seen
- You only use Windows and don't care about cross-platform
- You're comfortable with Microsoft's surveillance model
- You're willing to give up device storage for screen capture

**Choose Mirror if:**
- You want your AI to actually understand your context
- You care about privacy and security
- You use multiple AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.)
- You want memory that works proactively, before you ask
- You want a unified memory across all your devices
- You don't want to sacrifice device storage

Recall represents a surveillance approach to memory. Capture everything, store it, search it. It's been rejected by users repeatedly because it's intrusive and because it captures the wrong data.

Mirror is the alternative. We capture what matters structurally. We respect your privacy. We deliver understanding, not screenshots. And we do it in a way that actually helps your AI work better.
