# Mirror vs Granola: Meeting Notes vs. Meeting Intelligence

Granola is brilliant at what it does.

Granola raised $125M at $1.5B valuation in March 2026. Its bot-free approach, 90%+ transcription accuracy, and hybrid AI model that enhances sparse notes into structured summaries with action items have made it the meeting-notetaker of choice for VCs, consultants, and executives in back-to-back calendars.

Granola solves a real problem: capturing what happened in your meetings so you don't have to.

But there's a structural gap.

## The Gap: Meeting Transcripts vs. Relationship Intelligence

Granola gives you a transcript. Mirror gives you a briefing.

When you walk into your next meeting with a Granola user, they'll have perfect notes from the last one. When you walk in as a Mirror user, you'll have something more valuable: a relationship graph. The context graph includes the meeting transcript, yes. But also: every email exchange with that person over the last three months. Their stated priorities and commitments. The projects they're leading. The decisions they've made. How they think. What matters to them. How you've helped before. What you still owe them.

Granola is meeting-centric. Mirror is context-centric.

One tells you what happened. The other tells you who you're talking to—and why it matters.

## How Mirror Fills the Gap

1. **From Transcript to Relationship.** Granola transcribes. Mirror contextualizes. You don't need notes after the meeting; you need to know them before it. Mirror sits on three months of emails, calendar, Slack, and yes, meeting transcripts—and renders a personal briefing on demand. It's not "what was said." It's "who are they, what do they care about, and what do I need to know."

2. **Multi-Channel vs. Single-Channel.** Granola watches meetings. Mirror watches everything. Your real relationship lives across email, Slack, calendar, meetings, and documents. Granola sees one channel. Mirror integrates all of them into one graph.

3. **Pre-Meeting vs. Post-Meeting.** You can review what happened in Granola after the call ends. Mirror lets you know what matters before you walk in. The psychology is different. Preparation beats recap.

4. **Meeting-Specific vs. Continuous.** Every meeting is its own island in Granola. Mirror holds your entire relationship history with someone in one place. Cross-meeting patterns emerge. Commitments compound. You see momentum.

5. **Meeting Notes vs. Meeting Prep.** Granola's Recipes turn meetings into documents. Mirror turns relationships into intelligence. Documents are artifacts. Intelligence is actionable.

6. **Mirror + Granola is the Stack.** If you run both, Granola captures the transcript; Mirror uses that transcript as one thread in a larger relationship graph. Mirror covers three months of Granola for activated users—giving you the context layer that Granola doesn't touch.

## Which One Is Right for You?

**Choose Granola if you:**
- Run back-to-back meetings and need perfect, structured notes
- Work in an environment where meeting capture is your bottleneck
- Want to push summaries and action items into your CRM (Notion, HubSpot, Attio)
- Prefer bot-free recording and don't need relationship context
- Are on a tight budget and only need meeting-specific functionality

**Choose Mirror if you:**
- Need to understand who you're talking to before the meeting starts
- Want relationship intelligence that spans email, calendar, Slack, and meetings
- Are preparing for high-stakes conversations with clients, investors, or partners
- Care about commitment tracking and relationship momentum across time
- Want a personal briefing on any relationship, any time, in seconds

## FAQ

**Can I use Mirror with Granola?**

Yes. They're complementary, not competitive. Granola handles what happens in the meeting. Mirror turns those meeting transcripts (and your emails, calendar, Slack) into relationship intelligence. Use both.

**Is Granola a competitor?**

Not in the way you think. Granola owns the meeting-notetaking category. Mirror owns the personal context layer. Granola is about what happened. Mirror is about who you're with and why it matters. Different problems, different solutions.

**Why would I need Mirror if I have perfect meeting notes?**

Because perfect meeting notes are still just notes. They don't tell you how this person thinks, what they've committed to across multiple conversations, or what you need to know before you walk in. Notes are historical. Mirror is relational.

**Does Mirror replace Granola?**

No. Mirror uses Granola's output (the transcript) as one input to a larger relationship graph. If you're already a Granola user, Mirror extends what you get from it—adding email context, calendar patterns, and relationship dynamics that Granola doesn't touch.

## The Bottom Line

Granola solved meeting capture. Transcription, summaries, action items—all solved. It's earned its $1.5B valuation.

But meetings are only one conversation. Your real relationships live across email, Slack, calendar, documents, and meetings. Granola sees the meeting. Mirror sees the person.

When you have perfect meeting notes but still walk into conversations unprepared—when you forget what someone committed to last month, or you miss the patterns in how they think—that's the gap Mirror fills.

This is Artificial Personal Intelligence. Trained on you. Not on humanity. The difference between knowing what was said in a meeting and knowing who you're talking to.
