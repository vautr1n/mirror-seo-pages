# Your Personal Memory Is Your Most Sensitive Asset

You wouldn't hand your diary to a stranger. You wouldn't share your relationship observations with a competitor. You wouldn't leave your email and notes on an unsecured server.

Yet that's what happens with most "personal" AI tools.

## The Problem: Your Memory Becomes Someone Else's Training Data

Here's what most AI companies do with your personal context:

- **Training data:** Your conversations, your strategy notes, your relationship observations—they become training data. Your private thinking improves someone else's model.
- **Analytics:** They see what you're working on, who matters to you, what you're worried about. They know your problems before you've solved them.
- **Retention:** They keep it. Forever. Even if you delete, backups exist. Institutional memory, at your expense.
- **Sharing:** Your data flows through integrations, partners, and resellers. You don't know where it goes.

This matters especially when you're sharing *personal context*—your relationships, your commitments, your patterns, your worries, your private thinking about people.

Your memory is more sensitive than your bank account. More sensitive than health records. Because your memory is the map of who you are and how you understand your relationships.

When you put that in someone else's system, you're trusting that company to protect it. Most don't. They're built on the assumption that your data is an asset they can monetize.

## Why This Matters Right Now

As AI becomes embedded in work, people share more personal context with AI systems.

But most AI companies' privacy policies are designed to maximize *their optionality*, not *your protection*.

- They claim "we don't train on your data"... but they retain it indefinitely
- They say "encrypted in transit"... but not at rest
- They offer "enterprise privacy"... as a paid feature, not a baseline
- They're "GDPR compliant"... compliant is the minimum legal bar, not leadership

If Mirror is your persistent memory layer—the system that knows you better than you know yourself—it can't be built on an assumption that you're the product.

## How Mirror Approaches Security

Mirror is designed from the ground up with a different principle: **You own your memory. We protect it. We never profit from it.**

**Here's our architecture:**

### Encryption

- **In Transit:** Military-grade encryption (TLS 1.3+) for all data moving between your devices and Mirror
- **At Rest:** Your data is encrypted in our systems using AES-256. We don't have access to the plaintext keys. You do.
- **Locally:** Roadmap includes on-device processing, so sensitive operations never leave your machine

### Domain Separation

- **Work vs. Personal:** Your memory is compartmentalized. Work context doesn't bleed into personal. You have fine-grained control over what's shared.
- **By Relationship:** You can choose what context is available for different conversations
- **By Data Type:** You can restrict certain integrations (e.g., emails are included, but direct messages are not)

### Data Minimization

- **We collect only what's necessary:** Your calendar to know when you're meeting. Your email subject lines to surface context. We don't collect email bodies by default.
- **You control what we see:** Every integration has granular permissions. You decide what data Mirror accesses.
- **We delete what we don't need:** Time-bound data (meeting notes from 2 years ago) gets flagged for deletion on your schedule

### No Training on Your Data

- **Your memory never trains our models:** Unlike most AI companies, we don't use your personal context to improve our system. Your privacy isn't our training data.
- **No secondary use:** We don't analyze your patterns to sell insights. We don't profile you.
- **No sharing:** Your data doesn't flow to partners, integrations, or third-party systems without your explicit consent.

### Institutional-Grade Security

- **Pursuing SOC 2 Type II certification:** Designed with SOC 2 compliance in mind, supported by annual third-party audits of our security practices
- **Regular penetration testing:** We hire security researchers to attack our system. If they find vulnerabilities, we fix them.
- **Responsible disclosure:** If security issues are found, we fix them first, then disclose.
- **Security team:** Dedicated security engineers, not security as an afterthought

### Your Rights

- **Data portability:** You can export all your memory in a standard format, at any time
- **Deletion:** You can permanently delete any or all of your data. We delete it. No backups. No retention.
- **Transparency:** You can see what data Mirror has about you
- **Right to audit:** If you need to verify how we're handling your data, you can request an audit

## Why This Matters for Your Relationships

Here's what most people don't realize: your memory about people is their memory too.

When you use Mirror, you're encoding how you understand your relationships. Your observations about people. Your patterns with them. Your private thoughts.

If that's stored on someone else's infrastructure, you're exposing your relationships to that company's security practices.

Mirror keeps that private. Your memory of people stays with you. That company doesn't get to see how you think about your boss, your partner, your customers, your competitors.

## The Shift: Privacy as Infrastructure

Most companies treat privacy as compliance. "Do we check the boxes?" "Are we GDPR compliant?"

Mirror treats privacy as infrastructure. It's not a feature. It's not an option. It's how the system is built.

This means:
- Slower in some ways (encryption has computational cost)
- More expensive in some ways (no ads, no secondary monetization)
- Better in all the ways that matter (you own your memory)

We're betting that people will choose a tool they can trust over a tool that's slightly faster.
