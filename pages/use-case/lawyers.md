# Mirror for Lawyers: Every Client, Every Matter, Every Detail

## The Morning

It's 8am. Deposition prep in two hours. Which witness said what in discovery? What did opposing counsel concede in last month's meet-and-confer? You have the file somewhere — three folders deep in your DMS, buried under email exchanges and redlined documents you haven't opened in weeks.

9:30am client call. This is the partner who's anxious about costs. What did you quote last quarter? What's the current billing status? When did you last update them? You open your email and scroll backwards through subject lines, trying to reconstruct the thread.

11am intake. The GC mentions a predecessor firm handled something related. What was your history with them? What went wrong, or what went right? You know you know this, but you have 10 minutes to remember.

Lunch is thin gruel. Afternoon: three back-to-back calls. M&A due diligence. Employment dispute with a new compliance issue. Patent matter with a filing deadline in six days. Each requires a completely different context. You're carrying 30 active matters and 200+ relationships in your head.

By 5pm you're exhausted. Not from billable work — from context switching.

## The Problem

Law is the most context-intensive profession on earth. Every matter is its own universe: parties, opposing counsel, dates, discovery deadlines, court orders, settlement discussions, prior agreements, witness names, exhibit lists, strategic inflection points. The context lives everywhere — in your DMS, your email, Slack channels, handwritten notes, your memory.

Switching between matters is brutal. You spend 15-20 minutes before each meeting just reconstructing what you already know. Associates spend 30-45 minutes. Partners managing 15-20 simultaneous matters spend 3-4 hours per week just context-loading. And the cost of missing context isn't just inefficiency. It's a dropped deadline. It's a client commitment you forgot you made. It's malpractice risk.

The DMS knows everything but surfaces nothing. Email search is a disaster. Slack context expires. Your calendar shows meeting titles, not briefings.

Mirror changes this.

## What Mirror Does for Lawyers

### Client meetings

Before every client call, Mirror generates a context brief: billing status and recent invoices, the last 5 communications with this client, any open commitments you've made to them, their stated priorities and concerns from previous conversations, any shift in tone or sentiment over time. You read it in 90 seconds. You walk in prepared.

### Deposition prep

You're prepping a witness two days out. Mirror traces every mention of that witness across all correspondence, discovery responses, prior testimony references, emails where they're mentioned, exhibits where their name appears. It resurrects the full context of everything that witness has said or been involved in — context resurrection for the specific issues at stake in that deposition.

### Matter switching

You have back-to-back calls: M&A deal, employment dispute, patent litigation. Each is a completely different practice area, different clients, different strategy. Mirror briefs you in 90 seconds per matter. You have the essentials: recent actions, key milestones, upcoming deadlines, last client communication, any open questions.

### Cross-referencing and patterns

Has this opposing counsel ever made this argument before in a different matter? Has this client raised this objection in the past? Mirror knows because it tracks across your entire practice context — not just one matter in isolation.

### New matter intake

The GC calls with a new issue. Mirror immediately surfaces your full relationship history with that company, past matters you've handled for them, who the key contacts are, their billing preferences, anything you've learned about how they work. You're not starting from zero; you're inheriting your own institutional knowledge.

### Associate briefing and knowledge transfer

You're assigning an associate to a matter you've managed for two years. Instead of a one-hour handoff meeting, Mirror generates a context gift — a curated briefing of the relationship, the strategic history, the client's communication style, the open issues, key players. The associate gets the context they need without consuming your time.

## The Math

Partners average 25-35 meetings and calls per week. Associates average 15-20. Assuming 20 minutes of pre-meeting context reconstruction per meeting:

- **Partner**: 30 meetings × 20 min = 10 hours/week of context loading
- **Associate**: 18 meetings × 20 min = 6 hours/week of context loading

Mirror reduces this to 90 seconds per meeting.

- **Partner**: 30 meetings × 1.5 min = 45 minutes/week
- **Associate**: 18 meetings × 1.5 min = 27 minutes/week

For a partner, that's 9 hours per week of recovered billable time. For a firm with 50 partners, that's 450 hours per week. Across a year, at blended rates, that's in the millions.

But the real value isn't just time. It's risk mitigation. It's walking into every meeting prepared. It's never missing a commitment. It's knowing your clients, your deals, your cases deeply because you have immediate access to everything you've ever known about them.

## FAQ

**Is my client data safe?**

Yes. Your context graph is encrypted end-to-end. It lives on your machine and syncs only to you. Mirror doesn't train on your data. We're not building a legal AI model that learns from your cases — we're building a context system that works for you. Built by people who spent over a decade at Azure building enterprise security at scale.

**Does Mirror integrate with our DMS?**

Mirror natively integrates with email, calendar, and Slack. DMS integrations (Westlaw, LexisNexis, various practice management platforms) are in development. We're prioritizing the highest-volume sources first because context density matters more than breadth.

**Can partners and associates share context?**

Each team member has their own context graph. But you can generate a context gift — a curated briefing of a matter that you share with an associate, a junior partner, or a colleague. The associate gets the context they need to contribute; the context graph stays yours.

**What about attorney-client privilege?**

Your context graph is yours alone. It's not shared with other users, not trained on, not used to train models. Privilege and work product remain protected because the system is built as a personal layer, not a collaborative platform.

**Does this work for solo practitioners?**

Yes. The value might be higher. A solo managing 50+ matters across multiple practice areas feels the context load most acutely.
