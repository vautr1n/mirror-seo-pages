# Mirror for Recruiters: Every Candidate, Every Conversation, Every Follow-Up

Monday: 12 candidate screens. Tuesday: 8 debrief calls with hiring managers. Wednesday: 6 offer negotiations, 4 reference checks, and a sourcing sprint. You're managing 50+ active candidates across 8 open roles. Each candidate has a story — their motivations, their concerns, their timeline, what they said in the last call, what the hiring manager thought. When you mix up two candidates' details, the relationship breaks. The offer vanishes. The referral dries up.

## The Problem: Context at Velocity

Recruiting is pure relationship management at velocity. You're juggling dozens of concurrent conversations, each with its own context, timeline, and emotional dynamics. Your ATS tracks stages. It doesn't track the nuance — what a candidate really cares about, what the hiring manager whispered after the debrief, which offer terms are flexible, what competing offers look like. That context lives in your email, your Slack DMs, your notes, and your head. Every missed detail is a missed connection.

The cost compounds. A candidate doesn't hear from you for four days, and they take another offer. A hiring manager asks "how did that last candidate feel about the compensation" and you can't remember. A reference call happens without you knowing what the candidate claimed about their impact. You're operating on incomplete information, and candidates feel it.

## What Mirror Does for Recruiters

Mirror builds your context graph — a living, connectable record of every candidate, every conversation, every commitment. Before every interaction, Mirror briefs you on what matters.

**Before every candidate call**: Mirror surfaces your last conversation summary, their stated concerns, compensation expectations, timeline, and what you promised to follow up on. No re-reading old emails. No forgotten commitments. You walk in prepared.

**Hiring manager debriefs**: After your call, Mirror connects the dots: what this hiring manager said about the last 3 candidates in this role, their stated bar, their team dynamics, what they're really looking for. When you sit down with the next candidate for this role, you know the actual criteria, not just the job description.

**Offer stage**: Mirror traces the full negotiation history — what was discussed, what was offered, what competing offers exist, what the candidate's real priority is. When you negotiate, you're operating on facts, not guesses.

**Pipeline context**: Across 50 candidates, Mirror knows who's waiting, who's going cold, who needs a follow-up today. Your context gift — that moment before a call where you remember what matters — arrives before every interaction.

**Reference checks**: Before calling a reference, Mirror surfaces everything you know about the candidate's claimed experience. You ask smarter questions. The reference call becomes validation, not discovery.

## The Math

50 candidates × 3–5 touchpoints each = 150–250 context switches per week. Every switch costs attention. Every missed detail costs a relationship. Mirror eliminates the switch. You stay in the conversation instead of searching for context.

## FAQ

**Does Mirror integrate with my ATS?**

Mirror works alongside your ATS. Your ATS owns the pipeline stage and the job req. Mirror owns the context — the human layer that your ATS doesn't capture. You can push key notes back to your ATS, but the real power is that Mirror becomes your staging ground for context before every call and decision.

**How do you handle candidate privacy and data?**

Candidate data is sensitive. Mirror encrypts all candidate information in transit and at rest. You control what you share and with whom. If you're passing candidate information across your recruiting team, you control that visibility. Mirror never uses your candidate data to train models or share with third parties. Your context graph is yours.

**Can my whole recruiting team see the same context?**

Yes. Mirror lets you share context with hiring managers, sourcers, and coordinators on a need-to-know basis. A hiring manager sees the debrief notes and negotiation history for candidates in their role. A sourcer sees prospect research but not offer details. You control the visibility. Teams stay aligned without overexposing sensitive information.

**How do I get started?**

Start with a single role or a few active candidates. Log your last 3–5 conversations with each candidate into Mirror. Tag the key context — what they care about, what they're concerned about, what they asked for in the last call. Then, before your next candidate call, Mirror will brief you. From there, it compounds. More conversations. More context. Better decisions.
