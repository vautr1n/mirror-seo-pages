# Mirror for Design Teams: Client History That Lives Beyond the Figma File

The client review is in 15 minutes. You designed three options. But wait — didn't they say something about not liking rounded corners? Or was that a different client? The brand guidelines doc is somewhere in the shared drive. The feedback from the last round was in a Slack thread. The original brief is in an email from two months ago. Your creative director remembers the client's unspoken preference — but she's on another call.

This is the reality of design work at most studios. You're not missing skills. You're missing context.

## The Problem: Context Scattered, Decisions Repeated

Design is deeply relational. Client taste, brand DNA, feedback patterns, political dynamics in the approval chain — this is all context that shapes creative decisions. But it lives scattered across systems: Figma comments, email threads, Slack, meeting notes, Google Docs, your CD's memory, notebooks from old kickoffs.

The cost is real. You present work the client already rejected. You miss feedback they gave three rounds ago. You waste cycles redesigning things that were already decided. New team members join mid-project and repeat questions. Client switching between accounts loses continuity. Your studio runs on institutional knowledge instead of accessible context.

A context graph changes this. Mirror builds one for your design practice.

## What Mirror Does for Design Teams

**Client review prep in 90 seconds.** Before every presentation, Mirror surfaces the context you actually need: last round's feedback, stated preferences, design direction decisions, who has veto power in the approval chain, constraints from the brief, what's evolved since kickoff. Your team walks into every meeting prepared.

**Feedback archaeology.** A client says they don't like something. When did they say it? In what context? What were you designing at that moment? Mirror traces it. You find the exact feedback, the rationale behind it, and what you learned.

**Multi-client switching.** Agency life means 5-10 clients simultaneously. Each with different taste, different processes, different politics. Mirror loads a client's world in 90 seconds. A designer switches from Account A to Account B and carries the full context with them.

**New team member onboarding.** A designer joins mid-project. Instead of two days of onboarding conversations, they get a context gift: the full client relationship, feedback history, brand preferences, decision rationale, and the human dynamics that shape the work.

**Unspoken rules emerge from pattern.** Some clients want conservative options first, then bold alternatives. Some want to be surprised. Some need the business case before the aesthetics. Mirror captures these patterns from conversation history and surfaces them automatically.

The result: your team stops repeating work. Your presentations land better because they're informed by actual client history. Your designers spend time creating, not context-hunting.

## FAQ

**How does Mirror integrate with our existing tools?**

Mirror connects to your native workspace: Slack, email, Figma comments, meeting recordings, Google Docs. It doesn't require new software or a new process. Your team works in the tools they already use. The context graph lives in Mirror and surfaces when you need it — in your email, in a pre-meeting brief, in Slack.

**Do we have to change how we work?**

No. Mirror learns from how you already communicate. It reads feedback, decisions, and preferences from the places they naturally exist. As your studio's context graph grows, Mirror becomes smarter about what matters for each client. The onboarding is zero disruption.

**What happens to confidentiality?**

Your client relationships are private to your workspace. Mirror encrypts all context and doesn't share data across clients or teams. You control what goes into the context graph and who has access to it.

**How long does it take to see the value?**

Your first context gift builds in the first week. A new designer joining a project can load client history in minutes instead of days. A creative director prepping a client review gets the context refresh in 90 seconds. The value compounds as your context graph deepens.

## Work Smarter, Not Longer

Design studios thrive on client relationships. But those relationships shouldn't live in a single person's memory. Mirror makes relational context accessible to your entire team. Your designers show up to reviews prepared. Your new hires onboard in days instead of weeks. Your studio scales without losing the nuance that makes good design good.

Ready to build a context graph for your design practice.
