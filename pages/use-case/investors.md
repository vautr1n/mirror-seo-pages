# Mirror for Investors & VCs

Tuesday, 10 a.m.: board meeting for Portfolio Co #4. You have 30 minutes before the call. What did the CEO commit to last quarter? Which metric is trending down? What did you advise them on in Q3? Was there a personnel decision you recommended that you need to check on?

Wednesday, 2 p.m.: six pitch meetings back-to-back. Founder #3 walks in. You recognize the name, but was this the person who pitched at SaaStr three years ago, or did you meet them through a warm intro six months ago? What's their technical background? Did your associate flag red flags on the previous meeting? What did they say their timeline was?

Thursday, 6 p.m.: LP dinner. This LP is your largest check from the last fund. They asked about your AI thesis last time you met. What did you say? What's changed? What fund performance metrics should you have at hand?

This is the work of an investor. Not pattern recognition alone. Not market timing alone. **Relationship intelligence.** Your edge is knowing the founder you met two years ago who's now ready to raise Series B. It's remembering the board conversation that shaped a portfolio company's product pivot. It's tracking the LP relationship that started cold and is now a $50M commitment. It's knowing which of your portfolio CEOs faced the exact problem the founder in your 3 p.m. meeting is pitching.

## The Problem

Your context is scattered. Thousands of emails. Hundreds of meetings. Dozens of board decks. Your own memory, which is good but incomplete.

No CRM captures relationship intelligence. Salesforce is built for transactional sales pipelines, not investment thesis. No notes system connects the dots. Notion is a document dump. Slack is ephemeral. Excel is linear.

You end up in meetings under-contextualized. You miss follow-ups. You don't remember what you said. You don't connect the pattern that would have shaped your last investment decision. You spend 20 minutes in a board meeting re-establishing facts you knew six months ago.

This is friction. It compounds.

## What Mirror Does for Investors

Mirror builds your relationship context graph. Every founder you've met. Every board decision and commitment. Every LP conversation and stated priority. Every pattern in your portfolio. One system. Always current.

**Board prep.** Before every board meeting, Mirror surfaces: what commitments the CEO made last quarter and their status, open action items from the last meeting, metric trends you've been tracking, the CEO's stated concerns, what you advised. The context you need in three minutes, not 20.

**Founder meetings.** Before a pitch, Mirror briefs you: how and when you met this founder, what your team said in previous conversations, their background and prior exits, similar companies already in your portfolio, the specific questions to ask based on your last conversation. You walk in contextualized. You ask better questions. You spot patterns.

**LP relationships.** Before an LP meeting, Mirror traces the relationship arc: fund performance discussions you've had, their stated concerns and investment criteria, what you committed to reporting, how performance has tracked against what you said. You remember. You follow through. The relationship deepens.

**Deal flow pattern matching.** "Have we seen a company like this before? What was our thesis?" Mirror knows. It tracks every founder conversation, every pitch, every decision across your full portfolio.

**Portfolio cross-pollination.** "Which portfolio CEO had this exact problem last quarter?" Mirror connects patterns. You introduce the right portfolio companies to each other. You source better solutions.

## FAQ

**Does Mirror compromise confidentiality between portfolio companies?**

No. Mirror compartmentalizes relationship graphs by access level. If you're on Board A and Board B, and they compete, Mirror surfaces context only within each board's container. You see your full graph, but portfolio companies see only their own.

**Can my investment team access the same context graph?**

Yes. Your associates, analysts, and co-investors can be given access to specific relationships and interactions. This standardizes context across your firm and ensures no deal dies because one person saw the founder's background and another didn't.

**Does Mirror integrate with my deal flow and CRM tools?**

Mirror works independently, but you can send it calendar invites, email threads, and meeting notes from your existing tools (Google Calendar, Outlook, Gmail, Slack). It extracts the relationship context and adds it to your graph. No rip-and-replace.

**How do we set this up?**

We start with your past six months of calendar, emails, and notes. Mirror builds your initial relationship graph. From there, it learns from every new meeting, email, and decision you feed it. Weekly or monthly, depending on your preference, you get a brief of upcoming meetings with context you need.
