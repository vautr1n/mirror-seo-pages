# Mirror for Consultants: Total Client Context

Monday: pharma client, regulatory strategy. Tuesday morning: fintech restructuring. Tuesday afternoon: retail brand launch. Wednesday: PE due diligence. You carry 5-8 client worlds simultaneously. Each one expects you to show up knowing everything. When you switch from the pharma deck to the fintech model, your brain needs 20 minutes to reload. By Friday, you've spent 8+ hours just context-switching.

## The Problem: Context as Your Real Work

Consulting is context arbitrage — you're paid for what you know about a client's world. But that context is scattered. Email threads with 12 stakeholders. Slack channels per engagement. Shared drives across three organizations. Meeting notes mixed with deliverable feedback. Relationship dynamics you've picked up informally. No system connects it.

So you reconstruct manually, every time. You hunt for the email where the CFO flagged that budget constraint. You reread the latest steering committee minutes to remember which workstream was flagged red. You prep for a call by scrolling through six months of Slack.

And the worst part: the partner who staffed you on this three months ago has context you've never seen. The associate who left last week took institutional knowledge with them. The client relationship lives in your head and a folder of documents. If you leave, it leaves.

## What Mirror Does for Consultants

**90-second client briefing.** You're switching from pharma to fintech. Mirror loads your full context in 90 seconds: every stakeholder and their priorities, open workstreams and their status, what you committed to last week, what's changed since the last meeting, risks flagged by leadership. No hunting. No mental reload time.

**Stakeholder mapping.** Mirror tracks every person in the client org — who said what, who drives which decisions, who cares about which workstreams, who has real influence versus formal authority. The informal stuff that makes or breaks an engagement. You see patterns: the CFO blocks budget changes, the COO moves fast on operational work, the board asks the same question every quarter. No surprises.

**Engagement handoffs.** New consultant joining your team on this client? Send a context gift. The incoming partner gets the full relationship graph, stakeholder map, decision history, and open items — not just a folder of decks. Ramp time drops from two weeks to two days. Knowledge stays with the engagement, not in your head.

**Pre-steering-committee prep.** Before the big client meeting, Mirror surfaces every decision made this quarter, every open item from previous meetings, every risk flagged. You don't miss the detail that costs the relationship. You know what's changed, what's stuck, what the client is waiting on from you.

**Cross-client pattern recognition.** You solved regulatory compliance for the pharma client. Now the fintech client hits the same wall. Mirror surfaces how you solved it before and maps it to the fintech structure. Hours of thinking compressed into minutes.

## Briefing Example

Before your Tuesday morning fintech call:

> **Fintech Restructuring — Tuesday 9 AM**
> 
> **Key stakeholders:** CFO (budget-constrained, moves slow), COO (operational owner, wants decisions fast), CRO (compliance gatekeeper), Board (wants revenue plan).
> 
> **Last meeting (3 days ago):** You committed to headcount model and cost scenario by today. CFO flagged concern: "payroll restructuring hits August, not Q3." You said you'd model both.
> 
> **Open items:** Headcount model (yours, due today), cost-benefit analysis (CFO's, overdue), org chart (COO's, waiting on headcount).
> 
> **What changed:** CEO sent memo pushing go-live to August (was July). This affects three workstreams.
> 
> **Risk:** CFO's budget constraint is tighter than stated. Look for unstated constraint in today's call.

You walk in ready. No mental reload. No missing the detail.

## The Math

5-8 clients × 5-10 meetings each = 25-80 context switches per week.

At 20 minutes per context switch (finding the right docs, reloading names and relationships, remembering what you committed to): **83 to 267 hours per week lost to context-switching.**

Mirror cuts that to 90 seconds per switch. You recover 80+ hours per week. That's two weeks of billable work per week, reclaimed.

## FAQ

**How does Mirror keep client contexts separate?**

Each client engagement is isolated. Your pharma context stays locked to that engagement. Your fintech context is separate. You can only see what you've been staffed on. No cross-contamination, no accidental leak. Data separation is cryptographic.

**Can my team share context on a client?**

Yes. When a new consultant joins, you send a context gift — the full engagement context, unfrozen. They get access to everything you've built. When they leave, you revoke it. Context stays with the engagement; it doesn't scatter across the organization.

**Does Mirror integrate with our document management system?**

Mirror ingests your existing systems: email, Slack, shared drives, meeting recordings, deliverable feedback. It builds a unified context graph across all of them. You don't add another tool; you add a reasoning layer on top of what you're already doing.

**How long does setup take?**

30 minutes. Connect your email, Slack, and shared drive. Mirror ingests your existing engagement context. By tomorrow morning, you have a 90-second briefing for each client. No manual data entry. No weeks of configuration.
