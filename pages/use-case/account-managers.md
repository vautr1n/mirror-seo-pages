# Forty Accounts. Every Client Thinks They're Your Only One.

Your QBR is in 30 minutes.

This client — $200k ARR, signed 18 months ago. They escalated something in January. Your CSM handled it. What was the resolution? Their renewal is in 60 days. The champion who signed the deal left last quarter. The new VP has different priorities. You have 15 minutes to reconstruct 18 months of relationship context from scattered emails, Slack threads, and CRM notes.

You stare at the CRM. The data is there: deal size, close date, next renewal date. But where's the story? What actually happened in January? What did you promise them? What do they care about? What's changed about their business since last quarter? What's the new VP's style?

You flip through emails. Each one is a snapshot, not a narrative. You find the January escalation thread, skim it, find something about implementation delays. You open Slack. The account channel has 47 unread messages. You scroll back to January, reconstruct the incident (seems like it was a data integration issue, maybe fixed?), and surface in the call with 60% context.

Fifteen minutes later, they mention something about the fix and whether it "held up." You realize you don't actually know if the implementation succeeded.

This is what it looks like to manage 20-40 accounts at scale.

## The Problem: Relationship Data Scattered, Context Lost

Account management is relationship management at scale. You're juggling 20-40 accounts, each with 3-8 stakeholders, each with a history of conversations, escalations, commitments, and expectations.

Your CRM has the deal data. It tells you ARR, close date, renewal date, contract terms, maybe a few opportunities.

It doesn't tell you the relationship data. What the VP cares about most. What the engineer complained about in Slack. What was promised in the last call that never made it to a Salesforce note. Who's warm, who's cold, who's frustrated. What changed when the champion left.

That context is the difference between a renewal and a churn. Between a $200k account staying flat and expanding to $250k.

And you're reconstructing it manually before every call.

Multiply that by 40 accounts × 4 QBRs per year, plus weekly check-ins, weekly executive updates, ad-hoc renewal conversations, escalation triage. That's 200+ client touchpoints per year, each one requiring you to excavate your own relationship history.

The exceptional AMs you know? They don't scramble. They walk into every QBR remembering exactly what was promised, exactly what was delivered, exactly what's changed. They know who's frustrated. They know who the new decision-makers are and what they care about. They know what triggered the last escalation and whether it's actually resolved.

They look prepared because they are.

## What Mirror Does for Account Managers

Mirror maintains your full client context graph. Every conversation, every commitment, every stakeholder change, every signal — woven together so you can access it in seconds.

**QBR Preparation**: Thirty minutes before a call, Mirror delivers a briefing that includes:
- Full relationship history for every stakeholder (not just the original buyer)
- Escalation timeline and resolution status
- Commitment tracking: what you promised, what they promised, what's been delivered
- Sentiment analysis across all conversations (are they satisfied? Frustrated? Shifting priorities?)
- Usage patterns and adoption trends (if integrated with product data)

**Renewal Intelligence**: Before a renewal conversation, Mirror surfaces:
- Original value proposition and what drove the deal
- Delivery against every promise made at signature
- Open issues and their status
- Stakeholder changes (who left? who arrived? what do they care about?)
- Competitive threats and why they'd switch
- Expansion signals (new use cases, budget conversations, team growth)

**Stakeholder Transitions**: When a champion leaves or a new VP arrives, Mirror gives you what that person actually said in your previous conversations, what they care about, what style they prefer. You don't meet them as a stranger; you meet them knowing their context.

**Expansion Signals**: Mirror identifies patterns that signal upsell opportunity — increasing usage, new department mentions, budget conversations, scaling problems, team growth. You spot expansion before your CSM does.

**Relationship Health Tracking**: Mirror monitors sentiment across conversations over time. Is this account warming up or cooling down? Are your stakeholders engaged or disengaged? You don't learn about churn risk after it's too late.

**Handoff Context**: Account changing hands? Mirror generates a "context gift" — the full relationship graph, not just CRM notes. Your replacement walks in understanding 18 months of nuance in 15 minutes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Mirror integrate with my CRM?**

Yes. Mirror reads your Salesforce account history, calendar, email, and (if you use them) Slack, email, and Granola/Fireflies call transcripts. It connects the narrative threads that your CRM leaves scattered. All data stays in your control; Mirror doesn't replace your CRM, it completes it.

**What happens when an account changes hands?**

Mirror generates a context gift: everything the new AM needs to know about the account's history, stakeholders, commitments, and relationship health. The new AM walks in prepared instead of starting from zero. Account continuity improves immediately.

**How does Mirror handle data privacy?**

Your account data is private by default. You control what gets synthesized and who sees it. Context can be separated by role (executive gets a different briefing than CSM) and sensitivity (confidential conversations stay confidential). No data leaks across accounts.

**How long does setup take?**

60 seconds. Connect your CRM, email, and calendar. Mirror ingests your existing account history and starts building relationship graphs immediately. Your first QBR briefing is ready before your next call.
