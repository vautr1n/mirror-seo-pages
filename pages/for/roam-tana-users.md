# Mirror for Roam & Tana Users

Roam Research and Tana are thinking tools for a specific kind of mind — the kind that sees connections between ideas. Bi-directional links, networked thought, the ability to zoom in and out of relationships. Daily notes that compound into a graph of your thinking.

If you use these tools, you've already made a choice: **connections matter more than linear narrative**. You're willing to invest time in capturing ideas in a structured way because you know the payoff is exponential. The more you write, the more your thinking tool understands what you're thinking about. Connections emerge. Patterns show up.

Mirror brings this same philosophy to the context you're living in — not just the thinking you're capturing.

## The Gap: Capturing vs. Compounding

Roam and Tana users are disciplined. You write things down. You tag them. You maintain daily notes. Your tool builds a map of your thoughts through your attention and intention.

But there's a gap between the thinking you intentionally capture and the actual relationships that are shaping your work:
- Your meeting yesterday included a conversation that contradicts something you wrote down two weeks ago
- Three projects you're tracking separately share a common dependency that hasn't been explicitly stated
- A person you keep meeting with has a pattern of constraints you've started to notice
- A topic that seemed disconnected last month has become critical this month

These connections are real. They're shaping your decisions. But they're not in your Roam or Tana graph because they live in emails you didn't file, meetings you didn't transcribe, conversations that happened in passing.

You're capturing the connections you think are important. You're missing the connections that actually matter.

## How Mirror Complements Roam & Tana

Mirror captures the relational graph from your actual context — emails, meetings, calendar — and surfaces it as structured, queryable knowledge. It's the same philosophy as Roam and Tana: relationships are more meaningful than isolated facts.

The difference: Mirror discovers relationships automatically.

Here's what it means:

**Automatic Relationship Discovery** — You manually link ideas in Roam or Tana because you see they're connected. Mirror identifies the same kinds of connections in your email, meetings, and calendar automatically. You don't have to remember. You don't have to be disciplined about writing it down. The connections are already there, waiting to be surfaced.

**The Unwritten Graph** — Your Roam or Tana graph is a graph of thinking. Mirror builds a graph of doing. Your meetings create relationships. Your email threads create clusters. Your calendar shows who matters and when. This graph is as rich as your Roam vault — it's just been building itself while you lived your professional life.

**Just-in-Time Context** — You're in Roam working on a complex idea. You've linked related notes and pulled in references. But you don't remember that in a meeting three weeks ago, someone mentioned an assumption that contradicts your current thinking. Mirror knows. It surfaces it: "James raised this exact question on February 18. His concern was about feasibility constraints."

**Temporal Intelligence** — Roam and Tana excel at spatial relationships (how ideas connect). Mirror adds temporal intelligence. What changed between when you first discussed something and now? What was the sequence of decisions that led here? Who said what when? The narrative of your work emerges not from what you wrote, but from what actually happened.

**Consensus Mapping** — In Roam you write down what *you* think. Mirror captures what your team and stakeholders *said*. You're building a shared graph of understanding across your team, automatically, without asking anyone to maintain a wiki.

## Before and After

**Before Mirror:**
- You open Roam or Tana to develop a complex idea or decision
- Your graph shows connections you intentionally captured
- But you're missing context from meetings, emails, and conversations
- You switch to email or Slack to hunt for what people actually said
- You try to remember who raised what objection
- You might transcribe important context back into your tool (overhead) or work without it

**After Mirror:**
- Mirror has been automatically building a relational graph from your professional context
- You open Roam or Tana to develop the same idea
- Mirror surfaces the context from your meetings, emails, and conversations
- "This decision intersects with the vendor discussion (Sarah's email, March 10), the budget constraint (team meeting, March 15), and the technical feasibility question (James flagged this on March 8)."
- Your intentional thinking graph is now enriched with the automatic context graph
- You see connections between your thinking and your actual work
- You work from complete information without needing to manually capture everything

## What Doesn't Change

Your Roam or Tana vault remains what it is — your captured thinking, your intentional maps, your reference system. Mirror doesn't replace it or change how you use it.

Mirror simply ensures that when you're thinking in Roam or Tana, you have access to a parallel graph — the relationships that have been forming automatically in your emails, meetings, and calendar.

You're layering an automatic context graph under your intentional thinking graph. They inform each other.

## The Philosophy

Roam and Tana users understand that breakthrough thinking happens when you can see all the relevant connections at once. You've invested in tools and discipline because you know connection-rich thinking is more powerful.

Mirror extends that insight. The most important connections in your professional life aren't just between ideas. They're between what you think and what's actually happening. Between your plans and your constraints. Between your intentions and the context you're working in.

Mirror brings the relational thinking of Roam and Tana into your professional reality. Not by replacing your thinking tools, but by automatically surfacing the relationships you're living in.
