# Mirror for Google Calendar Users

Google Calendar is where your life lives. It's the single source of truth—every meeting, every commitment, every time block.

But Google Calendar tells you *when*. It doesn't tell you *why it matters*.

**Your calendar tells you when. Mirror tells you why it matters.**

## The Gap: Your Calendar Is Silent About Context

You open Google Calendar on Monday morning. Here's what you see:

- 9am: Design Review
- 10:30am: 1:1 with Sarah
- 12pm: Lunch (blocked)
- 1pm: Exec Standup
- 2:30pm: Customer Call
- 4pm: Board Prep

Google Calendar answers one question perfectly: "What time am I busy?"

But it doesn't answer the questions that actually matter:

- Who's in that design review, and what are they known for caring about?
- Sarah's 1:1—what did we talk about last time? Does she have concerns I need to address?
- That customer call—what's their history with us? What deal stage are they at? What are they evaluating?
- Board prep—what metrics do they care about? What concerns did they raise last time?
- The exec standup—who's likely to ask hard questions? Who wants something from me?

Google Calendar is the scheduler. It's not the context engine.

So every Monday, you open your calendar and see what's happening. Then you spend the next two hours reconstructing why it matters. Searching email. Checking Slack. Reading old notes. Trying to build context.

You're maximally scheduled but minimally prepared.

## How Mirror Closes It: Every Event, Automatically Contextualized

Mirror integrates with Google Calendar to add the context layer that Calendar can't carry.

**Every event on your Google Calendar gets enriched with:**

- **Attendee History:** Full context about everyone in the meeting—your relationship, previous interactions, what you know about them
- **Relationship Temperature:** How well do you work together? Natural rapport or known friction? Communication preferences?
- **Open Commitments:** What did you promise to deliver? What did they promise? Is everything on track?
- **Topic Context:** Not just "Meeting Title" but why you're really meeting—the actual underlying agenda
- **Preparation Materials:** Relevant emails, meeting notes, project docs, previous decisions. All surfaced in one place.
- **Recent Changes:** Since your last interaction, what's new? New priorities? New concerns? New opportunities?
- **Recommended Talking Points:** What should you bring up? What should you listen for? What would help them?
- **Post-Meeting Actions:** After the call, Mirror captures what you committed to

It's automatic. The moment you open an event, Mirror surfaces context. It transforms your calendar from a *schedule* into a *briefing system*.

## What Doesn't Change

Google Calendar stays Google Calendar. You use it exactly as you do now. Nothing changes about how you schedule or manage your calendar.

Mirror runs invisibly alongside, reconciling all the context from your emails, notes, Slack, and previous meetings. When you open an event, context is ready.

- You apologize, you have a solution
- You rebuild trust instead of losing it

- Wednesday 2pm: Mirror flags: "Sarah's been quiet this week. In last month's 1:1, she mentioned stress about technical debt. Your team just backlogged her cleanup work. She might be frustrated."
- You proactively ask her in standup. You learn the real issue. You adjust priorities.
- Issue surfaces early, before it becomes a morale problem

- Friday 4pm: Mirror brief shows: "Skip-level with Sam. Last call: Sam mentioned they're frustrated with their current project fit. No follow-up happened. This might be an early warning sign."
- You ask Sam directly how they're feeling
- You learn they're thinking about moving teams
- You have time to address it before they leave
