# Mirror for Granola Users

You already know Granola is exceptional at what it does. The transcription is accurate. The AI notes are sharp. You can edit them, cite them, build on them. If you're like most Granola users—founders, VCs, recruiters, sales leaders—you've probably wondered how to get *more* from those notes.

The answer isn't a better note-taking tool. It's context.

## The Gap: Transcripts Without Connection

Granola tells you *what was said*. But it doesn't tell you *what it means*.

You're sitting in a meeting with a founder you met 18 months ago. Granola transcribes beautifully. But you don't have immediate access to: their last three emails to you, the commitments you made in your previous meeting, whether they've been responsive to intros, what they're likely concerned about right now, or how this conversation connects to other deals you're working on.

You're taking notes. But you're also reconstructing context in real time—the backstory, the relationship temperature, the stakes. That overhead steals your attention from actually listening.

After the meeting, Granola gives you a great transcript. But connecting it backward to previous conversations, emails, and decisions? That's on you.

## How Mirror Fills the Gap

They're adding powerful new features—Spaces for team workspaces and Recipes for automated workflows—that make their transcription engine even more essential. **Scenario 1: Pre-Meeting Briefing**
Before your Granola-recorded meeting, Mirror pulls your briefing: who this person is, your interaction history with them, open commitments you made, their communication style based on past emails and meetings, current projects that might be relevant. You walk in fully contextualized—not scrambling to remember, not worried you've forgotten something important. You listen better. You ask better questions. Your Granola notes capture a sharper conversation.

**Scenario 2: Connected Notes**
After the meeting, Granola has your transcript. Mirror has connected it. It shows you which prior commitments were addressed, which new ones emerged, how this conversation relates to people or projects mentioned in previous meetings, what changed about your understanding of this relationship. Your Granola notes aren't isolated—they're threaded into your professional story.

**Scenario 3: Relationship Intelligence**
Over time, Mirror builds a knowledge graph of your relationships. Who tends to follow up quickly? Who prefers email to calls? Which relationships have shifted in tone? Which commitments are overdue? When you're about to record a Granola meeting, Mirror serves you the signals you need. When you review the transcript afterward, Mirror shows you whether the relationship trajectory is where you expect it to be.

**Scenario 4: The Dopel (Your Digital You)**
Mirror's Dopel agent can ingest your Granola transcripts and use them to draft follow-ups, flag action items in context, or prepare materials for your next meeting with this person. It's not just remembering—it's acting on your behalf, using the full weight of your context.

## Before and After

**Before Mirror:**
You have a 45-minute meeting with a potential investor. Granola records and transcribes it perfectly. You get detailed notes. But you spent the first 10 minutes of the meeting reorienting: *Have they invested in this space before? Did they mention a different thesis last time? What was the timeline they wanted?* After the meeting, you file the notes. Three weeks later, you're in a follow-up meeting and you realize you forgot one of their key concerns. You have the transcript—but you have to manually search it and connect it to context from the previous conversation.

**After Mirror:**
You have the same 45-minute meeting. But 15 minutes before it starts, Mirror sends you a briefing: this investor's prior bets, key themes in their recent communications with you, your last conversation summary, and open questions they raised. You're fully present in the meeting. Granola captures a sharper conversation. Afterward, Mirror shows you how this meeting affects your deal status, flags new commitments, connects this conversation to other investors you're talking to, and suggests what to focus on before the follow-up. Your Granola notes aren't just a transcript—they're woven into your strategic narrative.

You keep Granola. You keep using it exactly as you do now. Mirror sits alongside it, adding the context layer that Granola isn't designed to provide. Granola handles transcription, editing, and note-taking brilliance. Mirror handles memory, relationships, and the meaning behind the words.
