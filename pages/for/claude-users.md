# Mirror for Claude Users

Claude is the best reasoning engine on the market.

If you use Claude regularly, you know: the quality of thinking is exceptional. Claude doesn't just answer questions — it helps you *think*. It breaks down complex problems. It challenges assumptions. It notices edge cases. It writes with clarity and depth. For knowledge work, Claude is a competitive advantage.

But Claude has no idea who you are.

It doesn't know your name, your company, your team, or what you promised in yesterday's meeting. It doesn't know what your constraints are, who you need to convince, what your timeline looks like, what you've already tried. It's reasoning in a vacuum.

You're working with the best thinking engine available, but you're handicapping it by only giving it partial information.

## Why Claude Needs Your Context

Claude's strength is reasoning. But reasoning is only as good as the information it's reasoning about.

Ask Claude to help you think through a strategic decision. It will give you brilliant frameworks. But it doesn't know:
- Who are the stakeholders?
- What have you already committed to?
- What's your actual timeline?
- What constraints is your team under?
- What have you tried that didn't work?
- Who needs to buy in?

Claude has to ask you. Or you have to remember to tell it. Either way, you're not getting full leverage.

## What Mirror Adds to Claude

Mirror is the information layer that completes Claude.

Mirror watches your work — your emails, calendar, meetings, notes, Slack. It builds a comprehensive understanding of:
- Your relationships and how they're evolving
- Your commitments and their status
- Your projects and where they actually stand
- Your decision-making constraints
- Your communication style and patterns
- What you've tried and what worked

When you use Claude with Mirror:
- You don't have to explain your background
- Claude understands the actual constraints you're operating under
- Claude knows who you need to convince
- Claude understands the decision you're actually trying to make

Claude's reasoning becomes *personal reasoning* instead of generic reasoning.

## Claude + Mirror: The Combination That Elevates Everything

Think about what makes Claude powerful: it helps you think better by forcing you to be precise about what you actually know and don't know.

But there's a gap in that process. Claude reasons from what you tell it in a conversation. It doesn't know what you *should* tell it because it doesn't know what matters in your actual situation.

Mirror fills that gap.

With Mirror:
- Claude knows what actually matters to you
- Claude can reason about real constraints, not hypothetical ones
- Claude's advice is grounded in your actual situation
- Claude remembers context across conversations (not just within one chat)

The result is that Claude becomes less of a smart tool and more of a true thought partner.

**Strategic thinking is grounded in reality.**
You ask Claude to help you think through a big decision. Claude already knows: your team structure, your timeline, what you've committed to, what the board is focused on, what your customers are asking for. Claude's thinking accounts for your actual situation, not a hypothetical one.

**Writing is tailored to your audience.**
You ask Claude to draft something important — a proposal, an email to stakeholders, a strategic document. Claude knows your audience: what they care about, what they need to hear, what language resonates with them. The writing is specific to *your* situation, not generic.

**Problem-solving is faster.**
You describe a problem you're facing. Claude doesn't have to ask clarifying questions because it already has the context. It knows what you've tried, what resources you have, who can help. The solution is faster because the thinking is grounded.

**Research is actually useful.**
You ask Claude to research something. Claude researches not just the topic, but the topic *as it applies to your situation*. It surfaces what's most relevant to you, not just what's interesting.

**Advice is actually actionable.**
Generic advice is hard to act on. Claude's advice with your context is specific. It knows your constraints. It knows what's feasible. It knows who needs to be involved. It's not "here's what companies typically do." It's "here's what you should do given your specific situation."

**Follow-ups are proactive.**
You've been thinking about something with Claude. Weeks later, Claude proactively brings it back: new information has emerged that's relevant. Market conditions have changed. One of your stakeholders did something that affects your decision. Claude is thinking about it even when you're not.
