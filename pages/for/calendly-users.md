# Mirror for Calendly Users

Calendly solves one of the hardest problems in modern work: finding time.

No back-and-forth emails. No "Tuesday is bad, how's Wednesday?" No scheduling anxiety. You share a link. The other person picks a slot. Boom. Meeting scheduled.

Calendly removed friction from the *booking* process. But it doesn't remove friction from the *preparation* process.

**Calendly schedules the meeting. Mirror prepares you for it.**

## The Gap: The Meeting Is Booked, But You're Not Ready

Calendly nails the logistics. The meeting time arrives, and you're blocked off.

But you're not prepared:

- Who is this person? You booked the call weeks ago. Your memory of them is vague.
- What did you talk about last time? You can't find the notes. The context is scattered.
- What were you supposed to follow up on? Did you do it? Is it awkward that you didn't?
- What's your actual relationship temperature? Do you click easily, or is there friction?
- What should you ask them about? Recent wins? Current challenges? Are they still working on what they mentioned last time?
- What context would help this conversation be great instead of just transactional?

Calendly handles logistics perfectly. It leaves you unprepared for the human part.

So 30 seconds before the call, you're frantically searching email for context. Ctrl+F on message threads. Checking Slack. Trying to remember. You dial in feeling unprepared, asking clarifying questions at the start when you should be diving deep.

The other person senses it: "You probably don't remember, but..."

Calendly booked the meeting. You're winging the conversation.

## How Mirror Closes It: Automatic Briefings for Every Meeting

Mirror works alongside Calendly to deliver automatic briefings. When Calendly schedules a meeting, Mirror prepares you for it.

**For every Calendly event, Mirror delivers a briefing:**

- **Who is this person?** Full relationship history, previous calls, what you know about them
- **What did you talk about last time?** Previous meeting notes, decisions, outcomes, open threads
- **What's on their mind?** Clues from recent emails, Slack messages, shared context
- **What should you mention?** Recent wins of theirs, challenges in their space, opportunities to help
- **What changed since last time?** New developments in their world, new constraints, new information
- **Relationship temperature:** How well do you work together? Natural rapport or known friction? Communication style?
- **What to ask:** Thoughtful questions based on their priorities and your history

It's automatic. It's triggered the moment Calendly schedules. And it meets you exactly where you need it—minutes before the call.
