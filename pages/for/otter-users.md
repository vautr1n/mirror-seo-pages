# Mirror for Otter Users

Otter.ai is exceptional at what it does. Real-time captions are crisp. Summaries are intelligent. You can search across months of meetings and find exactly what you need. If you're using Otter, you've probably made it central to how your team captures institutional knowledge.

The problem isn't Otter's transcription. It's that meetings exist in isolation.

## The Gap: Summaries Without Continuity

Otter excels at one meeting at a time. It doesn't hold the story across all your meetings.

You're heading into a quarterly business review with your board. Otter has transcripts from your last three check-ins. You can search them and find specific moments. But you don't immediately have: What was the primary concern they raised last quarter? How has that concern evolved? What commitments did they make? What commitments did you make that are coming due? Has the temperature of the relationship shifted? Which of their questions from Q1 are you still being asked?

Otter shows you the individual meetings. You're left to reconstruct the narrative manually.

After the QBR, Otter captures excellent notes. But showing how this quarter's conversation changed the arc of the relationship, or how it connects to conversations with other board members—that requires you to hold all that context in your head.

## How Mirror Fills the Gap

Mirror works alongside Otter, weaving transcripts into a coherent, evolving story. One meeting at a time, Otter's great. Across meetings, Mirror makes you unstoppable.

**Scenario 1: Pre-Meeting Context**
Before your Otter-recorded meeting, Mirror delivers a briefing in seconds: this person's key questions across all prior meetings, how their concerns have evolved, commitments they've made and whether they're tracking, your relationship temperature trend based on communication patterns. You're not rereading old summaries—Mirror gives you the narrative. You walk in fully prepared, and ready to listen rather than reconstruct.

**Scenario 2: The Meeting Story**
Here's what shifts: Otter captures the transcript. Mirror captures the context from your recorded meetings. It shows you: which prior concerns were addressed, which new ones emerged, how this conversation relates to other meetings with this person (and with others), whether the relationship is on track or diverging from expected trajectory. Your Otter summary isn't filed away as an isolated artifact—it's woven into the continuous story of the relationship.

**Scenario 3: Pattern Recognition**
Otter transcribes meetings one at a time. Mirror holds the pattern across all of them. Over six months of Otter transcripts, Mirror shows you: how your relationship with this person has evolved, which topics keep surfacing (and whether they're getting closer to resolution), how their communication patterns have shifted, whether they're engaging more deeply or staying surface-level. You're not manually comparing transcripts. Mirror does it for you.

**Scenario 4: Institutional Memory**
Enterprise teams use Otter to build searchable knowledge. Mirror adds something else: it builds relational memory. Not just *what was said in meetings*, but *how your relationships are evolving across time*. When someone new joins your team, Mirror can brief them on the full context of an ongoing relationship in minutes, not hours.

## Before and After

**Before Mirror:**
You're an exec with a standing monthly with your CEO. Otter has transcripts from eight months of these meetings. You're preparing for month nine. You search Otter for the prior meeting, find it, and skim the summary. But you're not getting the arc: the concern they raised in month three that they've circled back to twice since then, the commitment you made in month five that's now overdue, the shift in their tone between month four and month seven. You're working from isolated transcripts, not from a continuous narrative.

**After Mirror:**
Same monthly, month nine. Two hours before the call, Mirror sends a briefing: here's what they've asked about across the eight prior meetings, here's what's evolved and what's stuck, here's what you committed to and the status, here's how the relationship arc looks. You're not skimming transcripts—you're walking in with the story. After this month's call, Otter captures it. Mirror immediately shows you: on track, accelerating, or stalled? What new concerns emerged? What changed? Your Otter transcript is threaded into the continuous narrative of the relationship.

## What Doesn't Change

You keep Otter. You keep using it exactly as you do now. Mirror sits alongside it, adding the connective intelligence that Otter wasn't designed to provide. Otter handles transcription, real-time captions, and per-meeting summaries. Mirror handles memory, relationships, and the story across all meetings.

This isn't replacement. It's continuity.
