# Mirror for Perplexity Users: Give Perplexity Your Context

## Perplexity Does Web Search Better Than Anyone

Perplexity is the fastest way to find answers on the internet. It searches the web, synthesizes information from dozens of sources, and returns a grounded, citations-included response in seconds. Millions of people use Perplexity for research, comparison shopping, competitive intelligence, and learning.

But every search starts from zero.

When you ask Perplexity "best CRM for my team," it gives you a generic ranking: HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, etc. It doesn't know your team is 12 people, your budget is $500/month, you already own Slack, your CTO hates Salesforce, or that you're migrating from Pipedrive this quarter. Perplexity has no context. So the answer, however well-researched, misses what matters to you.

## This Is Where Context Matters

Most of what you ask an AI depends on what the AI knows about you.

Your relationship with Sarah shapes how you should prepare for that meeting. Your hiring philosophy changes which interview questions you should ask. Your past decisions about cloud infrastructure inform which architecture recommendation makes sense for your next project. Your industry, your team size, your risk tolerance, your values — these are the axes that turn generic answers into useful ones.

Perplexity searches brilliantly. But it searches as if every user is the same.

## How Mirror + Perplexity Work Together

Mirror is a personal context layer. It builds a graph of your relationships, goals, projects, decisions, and priorities. When you connect Mirror to Perplexity (or any AI tool), that graph travels with you.

**Research with your context.**
You ask Perplexity "best CRM for my team." Mirror surfaces your context: team size, budget, existing tools, past decisions, hiring philosophy. Perplexity finds the information. Mirror makes sure the answer is relevant to you.

**Meetings become meaningful.**
You ask Mirror "prep me for my meeting with Sarah." It surfaces your relationship history, past conversations, her priorities, what you were working on last time. When you then search Perplexity or ask Claude about a topic relevant to Sarah, you're doing it with context.

**Relationships fuel discovery.**
You mention a competitor in conversation. Mirror flags it, stores it, tracks it. When you research that competitor in Perplexity, the search is grounded in your competitive context, not generic industry analysis.

**No setup. No friction.**
Mirror reads your natural language. You don't fill out forms or tag things. You say "I met with marketing today and they said the campaign is costing 40% more than budgeted." Mirror absorbs it. Next time you search Perplexity about marketing budgets or campaign economics, that context is there.

Perplexity is the best AI search engine. It finds answers from the web faster and more accurately than anything else. Mirror gives you a personal context layer. Together, you get answers that are both accurate and relevant to your life.

You're no longer asking an AI a generic question. You're asking an AI that knows you.
