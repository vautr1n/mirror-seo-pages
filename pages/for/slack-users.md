# Mirror for Slack Users

Slack is where work happens. It's also where work disappears.

Decisions made in threads get buried when the channel moves on. Commitments slip into DMs and vanish after seven days. Context fragments across 50 channels, half of them you're not even sure why you're in. By Friday, you've processed 500 messages and absorbed none of it.

Slack is a firehose. And firehoses drown.

## The Slack Problem: Signal Lost in Noise

You know the feeling. You're looking for something that was discussed last month. You *remember* it. But which channel? Which thread? What were the exact keywords? You search, you scroll, you give up.

Even worse: someone mentions a decision that was supposedly made. You have no memory of it. Did you miss it? Was it in a channel you don't follow closely? Was it in a DM you forgot about? Now you look uninformed, confused, or out of the loop.

This isn't a Slack problem. Slack does exactly what it's built to do: it's a real-time communication platform. It's optimized for speed and presence, not for memory.

**But your brain isn't optimized for a firehose either.**

## What Mirror Does to Slack

Mirror extracts the signal from Slack's noise.

Instead of drowning in messages, Mirror surfaces what matters:
- **The decisions** that were made (and by whom)
- **The commitments** people took on
- **The relationships** that are forming (who works together, what's the tone, what's the trajectory)
- **The open questions** that are waiting for answers
- **The context** of why something was discussed, not just that it was

Mirror doesn't replace Slack. You stay in Slack for real-time collaboration. Mirror is what makes Slack *useful* — it builds a coherent narrative from Slack's beautiful chaos.

## How Mirror Works with Your Slack Data

Mirror connects to Slack (with encrypted, secure permissions) and watches your channels and DMs. It doesn't index every message — that would be overwhelming. Instead, it's actively working:

- **Extracting commitments.** Someone says "I'll have the report ready by EOW." Mirror notes it, tracks it, reminds you. Did they deliver? Did the deadline move? Mirror knows.

- **Mapping relationships.** Over time, Mirror understands who collaborates, who depends on whom, what the communication patterns are. You see the evolution of each working relationship.

- **Tracking projects.** A project gets discussed across multiple channels, multiple DMs, multiple threads. Mirror stitches it together into a coherent picture: here's the state, here's what's pending, here's who needs what.

- **Building context.** When someone posts something today, Mirror already has the background. That cryptic message about "the Q2 thing"? Mirror knows what it means because it has the full history.

- **Intelligent searching.** You remember a decision but can't find it. Instead of searching Slack's firehose, you ask Mirror. It understands context, not just keywords. "What did we decide about the API redesign?" Mirror knows.

## Slack Is Real-Time. Mirror Is Coherent.

Slack is optimized for the moment. The right conversation happening right now, with the right people, in the right channel. That's powerful.

But moments blur. After a week, the moment is gone. After a month, it's archaeology.

Mirror is optimized for the long view. It holds the narrative. It remembers what you promised, what was decided, where each project actually stands.

Together:
- **Slack** gives you real-time collaboration and presence
- **Mirror** gives you the coherent narrative that Slack can't sustain

You communicate in Slack. You remember in Mirror.

## The Specific Problems Mirror Solves for Slack Users

**"Did someone promise this or am I making it up?"**
You have a vague memory of someone committing to something. Was it real? Mirror knows. It's built a graph of commitments across your Slack conversations. You can ask it directly: "Did engineering commit to the new dashboard by June?"

**"What was the context for this decision?"**
Someone references a decision made weeks ago. You're confused. Why did we decide *that*? What was the business case? Mirror has the context: the conversation that led to it, the alternatives that were discussed, the reasoning.

**"What's actually in progress across my team?"**
You have an intuition that something's stalled, but you're not sure what or where. Mirror shows you: across all your channels and DMs, here's what people committed to, here's the status, here's what's overdue or at risk.

**"Who do I need to loop in?"**
Someone asks about something that affects a project. You want to include the right people in the conversation. Mirror knows who's been involved with that project, who cares about it, who has expertise in it.

**"What do I need to follow up on?"**
Commitments get made and forgotten. Not maliciously — people are busy. Mirror tracks them. It alerts you when something is due, or overdue, or at risk.

**"What does this person actually care about?"**
You're about to have a conversation with someone. What matters to them? What are they working on? What's their style? Mirror shows you the pattern of their Slack communication: what they focus on, what they care about, what motivates them.

## Slack + Mirror = Complete Communication Intelligence

Slack gives you speed and presence. Mirror gives you memory and coherence.

Speed without memory is chaos. You're constantly relearning. Memory without speed is slow. Mirror is fast — it works in the background, proactively surfacing what you need, when you need it.

The result: **you communicate better because you have context**.

## How Mirror Integrates with Your Slack Workflow

You don't leave Slack. You stay in Slack, using it for what it's brilliant at. Mirror works alongside:

- **Pre-meeting briefings.** Before a call, Mirror surfaces the context: what's been discussed with these people, what's in progress, what's pending. You come to the meeting informed.

- **Daily intelligence.** Every morning, Mirror gives you a brief: what was decided yesterday, what commitments were made, what's at risk, what needs attention.

- **Proactive reminders.** Mirror knows what's due today. What follow-ups you need to send. What conversations you should revisit.

- **Context panels.** When you're in Slack, Mirror is there too — showing you the context of the current conversation without leaving Slack.

- **Searchable memory.** You can ask Mirror questions about Slack that Slack's search can't answer. "What's the status of the API project?" "Who owns customer feedback?" "What did we decide about the redesign?"

## Why Slack Users Choose Mirror

If you use Slack, you understand that communication is central to your work. But you also know that **communication without memory is just noise**.

Slack is built for connection. Mirror is built for continuity. Together, they're built for impact.

Your competitors might have Slack. They don't have your institutional memory. Your knowledge graph of decisions, commitments, and relationships. The coherent narrative that Mirror builds from Slack's beautiful chaos.
