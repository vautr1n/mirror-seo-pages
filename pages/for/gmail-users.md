# Mirror for Gmail Users

You have a decade of emails in Gmail.

Maybe more. 50,000 messages, or 100,000, or half a million. A complete record of every professional relationship you've built, every project you've worked on, every commitment you've made. It's an extraordinary archive of your professional life.

It's also a graveyard. Searchable, but dead.

Gmail can find a message if you know the right words. But it can't tell you what you promised. It can't remind you who needs what. It can't show you the evolution of your relationship with someone. It can't connect decisions made in January to promises made in September. It can't extract the commitments buried in prose or the context that makes a question make sense.

Your inbox is a gold mine. Gmail is a metal detector that only finds nails.

## What's In Your Email That You Can't See

Search for someone's name. Gmail will show you every email you've exchanged. That's a start. But there's so much more in there:

- **The trajectory of your relationship.** How did it start? How has it evolved? What's the tone — formal, warm, collaborative, tense? Has it changed over time?

- **What they care about.** Not what they say they care about. What they *actually* care about, based on what they ask about, what they emphasize, what they spend time on.

- **What you promised them.** Buried in email prose are commitments. "I'll get that to you by Friday." "We can look into that." "I'll send you the deck after the board meeting." Did you deliver? Did they follow up?

- **Open threads.** Conversations that started but never resolved. Questions that were asked and never answered. Projects that were discussed and then went quiet.

- **Your working style with different people.** You're more formal with some, more casual with others. You move quickly with some, slowly with others. You can't see these patterns until you have the whole picture.

- **Decision archaeology.** Three months ago, someone references a decision that was made. You don't remember it. Was it email? Was it a meeting and you're just being reminded? What was the reasoning? What alternatives were considered?

All of this is *in* your Gmail. You just can't see it.

## The Problem With Gmail Search

Gmail's search is powerful if you know what you're looking for. But most of the questions you actually want to answer aren't search queries.

You don't search for "commitments I made to Sarah." You just struggle to remember what you promised her.

You don't search for "my working relationship with this person." You just notice, in a meeting, that you're missing context.

You don't search for "decisions made in Q3 about the API." You just hope someone else in the meeting remembers.

Gmail is designed for *finding emails*. You need something designed for *understanding relationships*.

## What Mirror Does With Your Gmail

Mirror reads your email archive and builds what Gmail can't: a coherent knowledge graph of your professional life.

This isn't about reading every email. It's about extracting meaning:

- **Who you know.** Not just a list of addresses, but a real understanding. Since when? About what? Working relationship or friendship? Colleagues or collaborators? What's their role in your professional life?

- **What's in progress.** Across all your emails, Mirror finds the commitments, the open questions, the pending decisions. It tracks them over time. What was promised? When? Did it get delivered? Is it at risk?

- **The narrative of each relationship.** How did you and this person start working together? How has the relationship evolved? What defines your working style together?

- **Project context.** You've worked on 50 projects in the past decade. Mirror understands them: what they were about, who was involved, what the outcome was, what's still unresolved.

- **Historical context.** Someone mentions something from two years ago. Mirror knows: that refers to the Q1 2024 initiative about X, which was about solving the problem of Y, which was because of Z. Here's the full picture.

And critically: **Mirror makes all of this searchable and proactive.**

You don't have to dig. Mirror surfaces what you need when you need it.

## How Mirror Works With Your Gmail Data

Mirror connects to Gmail with secure, encrypted permissions. It doesn't spam you with alerts. Instead:

- **Pre-meeting briefings.** Before you talk to someone, Mirror gives you the context: your history together, what's in progress, what was unresolved, what they've been focused on lately.

- **Daily intelligence.** Every morning, Mirror highlights what's important: follow-ups that are due, commitments that are at risk, decisions that need your input.

- **Smart search.** You ask Mirror a question: "What did we decide about the logo redesign?" or "What does Sarah still need from me?" or "Who have I been collaborating with on infrastructure?" Mirror understands and answers — not just by searching keywords, but by understanding context.

- **Proactive reminders.** Mirror knows you promised something by Friday. It reminds you on Thursday. It knows someone asked a question that's still unanswered. It brings it back.

- **Relationship insights.** You're about to talk to someone you haven't emailed in six months. Mirror shows you: when you last worked together, what you were working on, what might have changed.

- **Commitment tracking.** Mirror watches for commitments in your emails and extracts them. You promised something. Mirror tracks it. You can ask: "What have I committed to that's due this month?"

## Email Without Context vs. Email With Context

With just Gmail:
- You have access to every word anyone ever sent you
- You can find a message if you remember the right keywords
- But you're always swimming upstream, trying to reconstruct context

With Gmail + Mirror:
- You have the same email archive
- But Mirror has extracted the meaning
- Context is proactive, available, searchable
- You understand relationships, not just messages

The difference is like having a filing cabinet full of documents vs. having someone who has read them all and understands your business.

## The Real Value: Your Email Is a Learning Machine

Here's what most people don't realize: your email archive is one of the richest datasets about your professional life. If it were decoded properly, it could teach an AI:
- How you think
- What you care about
- Who you work best with
- Your communication style
- Your priorities and values
- Your decision-making pattern

Mirror does that decoding. It doesn't just archive your emails. It *understands* them.

That understanding then flows into everything else Mirror does: when you're working with ChatGPT, Mirror gives it your context. When you're about to meet with someone, Mirror briefs you. When you're trying to remember something, Mirror knows.

Your email isn't just a record of what happened. It's the source material for intelligent decision-making.
