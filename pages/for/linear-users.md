# Mirror for Linear Users

Linear tracks tickets beautifully. Issues, cycles, roadmaps. It's the system of record for what gets done and who's doing it.

But here's what Linear doesn't track: the humans behind the tickets. The relationships, the verbal commitments made in meetings, the tensions simmering in Slack, the context that never makes it into a ticket description.

**Linear is the system of record for tasks. Mirror is the system of record for context.**

## The Gap: Tickets Without Tension

You open Linear. Your sprint is full. Priorities are clear. Assignments are crisp.

But beneath that clarity, there's complexity Linear can't see:

- Sarah's been assigned to this ticket for three days. But in Slack, she mentioned a blocker you haven't addressed. Is she stuck, or just quiet?
- Mike said in last week's meeting that this timeline concerned him. But his ticket shows no concerns. Did he change his mind, or is he waiting for someone else to voice it?
- This feature request came from Customer X. How important are they really? What else have they asked for? How much leverage do they have?
- The person assigned to review this is on vacation Thursday, and the timeline is tight. Does anyone know?
- You closed a ticket last month that's tangentially related. What actually shipped? What broke? Did anyone learn anything?

Linear shows you what needs to happen. It doesn't show you why it matters, who actually cares, or what context you need to move it forward effectively.

## How Mirror Closes It: Human Context on Every Ticket

Mirror enriches Linear by answering the questions Linear can't—the human questions that separate teams that ship from teams that thrash.

For every ticket, Mirror surfaces:

- **Who Actually Cares:** Not just who's assigned, but who's involved in related conversations. Who mentioned concerns? Who's been silent? Who's got leverage?
- **Relationship Temperature:** How do you actually work with this person? Strong rapport? Known tensions? Previous friction on similar work?
- **Open Commitments:** What did people say they'd do? What were they promised? Is there a mismatch between the ticket assignment and what was actually discussed?
- **Institutional Memory:** What happened last time we did this? What changed? What did we learn? What broke?
- **Hidden Blockers:** Is there a concern buried in a Slack thread? A constraint mentioned in a meeting that didn't make it to the ticket? A dependency that lives in someone's head?

It's not another column in Linear. It's a context layer that activates when you need to *actually* move work forward.

## Before and After

**Before Mirror:**
- Open Linear → See ticket assigned to John
- Assume he'll do it
- Check in after three days
- John: "Oh, I had a question about the approach, but I didn't want to bother anyone"
- Spend a week clarifying, aligning, resolving
- Context was there all along. It just lived in John's head.

**After Mirror:**
- Open Linear → See ticket assigned to John
- Mirror brief: "John cares deeply about this area. Last related project, he raised concerns about approach. You've addressed them in the approach doc (linked). He's in back-to-back meetings until Thursday, but this is on his radar."
- You proactively link the approach doc in the ticket
- You flag the specific improvements John cares about
- John reviews it in 30 minutes, no back-and-forth needed
- One day to resolution instead of ten

## What Doesn't Change

Linear stays Linear. We're not replacing it. We're not asking you to adopt another tool. You work exactly as you do now—tickets flow, assignments happen, work moves forward.

Mirror runs alongside your workflow, continuously reconciling context from email, Slack, calendar, and notes. It's always ready when you need to understand the human side of the work.

## Why Linear Teams Choose Mirror

Linear users understand that process matters. You've designed workflows that scale. You've optimized how work gets organized and tracked.

The next optimization is the humans. Mirror brings clarity to relationships and context the way Linear brought clarity to tickets and cycles.

You get:

- **Relationship Intelligence:** See how you actually work with each team member. Friction patterns. Collaboration flows. Communication preferences.
- **Commitment Tracking:** Mirror remembers what people promised—in meetings, in Slack, even in tone. When reality diverges, it flags it.
- **Historical Context:** Related tickets, past decisions, lessons learned. All surfaced in seconds, not in hunting through Linear history.
- **Proactive Alerts:** Blockers emerging? Misaligned commitments? Relationship friction about to derail a project? Mirror flags it before it becomes a crisis.
- **Context Resurrection:** Everything relevant to this ticket, from every source, brought together in one briefing.

## The Daily Brief: Your Team's Context in One Place

Each morning, Mirror delivers a summary of your day: who matters, what's at risk, what commitments are due. It's like having a team lead who knows everything about your relationships and projects.

Then when you open Linear, you're not starting blind. You know which tickets are about to hit friction. You know who needs support. You know what context is missing.

## Example: A Sprint Review Setup

**Without Mirror:**
- Friday: You're preparing for sprint review
- You see 12 completed tickets
- You're planning to showcase three features
- During the demo, a teammate raises a concern that clearly came from a meeting you missed
- Another person who was quiet the whole sprint suddenly has strong feedback
- You realize the context was there, but nobody surfaced it

**With Mirror:**
- Friday: Mirror's daily brief flags three tickets with stakeholder concerns
- You see that one of your quiet teammates had reservations they didn't voice in Linear
- You see that a decision made in last week's meeting created a misalignment nobody's talked about yet
- You have an hour to align the team before review
- Sprint review goes smoothly because context is pre-aligned

## Example: Managing a Critical Dependency

**Without Mirror:**
- Task A is blocked waiting for Task B
- Your teammate working on B has the task assigned
- You check in every couple days
- Nothing's happening. You're not sure why.
- After a week, you learn: they were confused about scope, they had a question about the approach, they got distracted by a higher-priority ask
- All of this was mentioned casually in Slack. You didn't know to look for it.

**With Mirror:**
- Task A is blocked waiting for Task B
- Mirror shows: "Your teammate has this assigned but mentioned scope questions in Slack. They're also juggling a higher-priority ask from [person]. Relationship temperature: strong, but they're overwhelmed. Best move: sync with [person] about their priority, then help your teammate scope Task B."
- You have a 15-minute conversation with both people
- Task B unblocks immediately
- No week-long stall
